A. truei is an ancient species in the comparatively young habitat of the Coast Mountains in B.C.



They are of conservation interest because large portions of its range are managed for timber harvest.



I conducted studies designed to understand the genetic connectivity and spatial ecology across the northern portion of its range.















First, a phylogeography across the northern half of the species’ range.



Second, an exploration into a few aspects of the ecology of larvae from stream reaches near the northern extent of the frog's range.



Then, concluding remarks.
