I found unexpectedly low genomic DNA diversity along the northern half of A. truei's range.



Geographic regions (N = 5) separated into 4 genetic clusters with the two most northern regions clustering together.



My results suggest that populations in British Columbia were derived from a single refugium during the Pleistocene glaciations.



I had a low number of geographic regions, limiting the scope of the work.
