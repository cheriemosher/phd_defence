Chapter 1. Tracking the northern range expansion using two types of genetic markers

I used microsatellite and genotype-by-sequencing to compare the within region genetic diversity of populations near the northern extent of the speciesâ€™ range (British Columbia, Canada) to two geographic regions in British Columbia and two in Washington, USA, moving towards the core of the range.




















The distribution of A. truei in BC and WA, USA is greatly influenced by the Pleistocene glaciation.


Ease of northern expansion is tied to dispersal ability, the number and distribution of historical refugia, and the availability of suitable habitat.























Objectives



Objective 1: Better understand the northern expansion of this species.


Objective 2: Determine if the GBS method provides better insight into the genetic diversities within and between geographic regions.

I found unexpectedly low genomic DNA diversity along the northern half of A. truei's range.



Geographic regions (N = 5) separated into 4 genetic clusters with the two most northern regions clustering together.



My results suggest that populations in British Columbia were derived from a single refugium during the Pleistocene glaciations.
