Conclusions

Curious Case of the Coastal Tailed Frog (Ascaphus truei) Near the Northern Extent of Its Rang

Comprehensive studies of the habitat and movement ecology of a species will aid in appropriate conservation actions to maintain long-term persistence.

I performed three studies at varying spatial and temporal scales near the northern extent of A. truei's geographic range.


I found a dramatic reduction in genetic diversity across the northern portion of the speciesâ€™ range.


Northern populations have been established since the late Pleistocene era.


Therefore, when considering conservation and management plans for A. truei, we should make sure we minimize the impacts our land use has on their movements throughout their landscapes.























Gut contents differed between sampled stream reaches.


My results suggest lotic invertebrates as likely prey.


Changes to stream structure that impact lotic invertebrates may have important implications for larvae.

Capture rates nonlinearly decreased as wetted width increased. Capture rates nonlinearly increased with depth.


Older larvae were associated with greater slopes than younger larvae.















Human activity that creates more uniform stream structure may have adverse impacts on the success of larvae.


We should prevent the direct modifications of stream structure.




















We must maintain a natural mosaic of clean fast-flowing streams and forests across these fog and rain-drenched mountains.
